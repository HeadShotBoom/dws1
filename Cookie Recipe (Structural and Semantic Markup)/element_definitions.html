HTML Element Definitions

<h1>
The heading at the top of the page
<h2>
Additional Heading (Smaller than previous heading)
<h3>
Additional Heading (Smaller than previous heading)
<h4>
Additional Heading (Smaller than previous heading)
<h5>
Additional Heading (Smaller than previous heading)
<h6>
Additional Heading (Smaller than previous heading)
<p>
Text entered in these tags will be treated as a single paragraph.
<ul>
Makes a list where each item is preceded by a dot
<ol>
Makes a list where each item is preceded by a dot
<li>
This indicates an individual item in either an ordered or unordered list
<strong>
Anything in here is important (BOLD)
<em>
Draws emphasis on text (Italics)
<blockquote>
Used when you want to quote something that is very long. Quotation marks arent used, indention is
<q>
This will put quotation marks around a word in a paragraph
<abbr>
This will allow you to abbreviate a word and include a definition in a tag
<cite>
Allos you to reference the source of any "Borrowed" material.
<dfn>
Allows a definition to be displayed for a word on your page
<address>
Allows you to store your contact info
<ins>
Allows you to show where edits have been made on a page. Used for new info. (This typically underlines the characters)
<del>
Allows you to show where edits have been made on a page. Used for info that is to be removed. (Strikethrough)
<s>
Pretty much the same as del but allows you to specify that the content is inaccurate.
